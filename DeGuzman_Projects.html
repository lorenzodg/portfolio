Scratch Project | August 2018 | Aiden Manansala | Lorenzo De Guzman | CSP

Our task was to create a game or story in Scratch. We immediately agreed on creating a story. Our tale was based on a human-like entity in another realm, attempting to find treasure inside of a old temple. The task inside the game was to choose options in order to find the lost treasure, awaiting inside the temple. In this project, we had a very trusted type partnership so there was no need for any type of role. We could bounce ideas back and forth to each other, discuss it, and then place the altercation into the story. We both displayed our strengths in creativity, efficiency, and collaboration better than I thought we would. In the end, we the only group to go down the path of creating a story. A lot of people had interest in our project, but there were faults. We had expected them but we knew we could fix the errors. Either way we had been satisfied with our work, and now we strive for exponential growth for future projects and work.

Image Manipulation Project | Febuary 2019 | Ethan Fernando | Lorenzo De Guzman | CSP

Our task provided was to manipulate an image fitting to the chosen scenario. First we had gotten into pairs, and after that we had done a couple activities about images and how to alter them. After we got used to each other and the concept, we had to choose our scenario. We had choices of making a family picture frame, advertise a political cause, or promote a new tool similar to Photoshop. We had chosen the last option, which was to promote a product that is prevalent in today's society. We brainstormed for a long time as there many things that our society needs. From new pans, to household items. We noticed the trend on gaming, so we decided on a gaming chair ready for anyone. Going down this path, we both realized each other's strengths and downs. We knew that we had lots of time, but also a lot of work. We continued to work but we didn't focus on the correct parts so we fell behind. We did make our parts up over the weekend and we finished the project. We had many errors in just planning itself, and more in coding and ways of coding. We finally learned that our skills and lessons were learned there, and the price was paid with the final verdict of grading. But, this is what motivates us and pushes us as scholars.
